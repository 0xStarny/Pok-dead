## 1. The Outbreak — What happened

### Origin (purely scientific, no mysticism)

**Dr. Mortimer**, a cellular biologist from Cinnabar Island and a former colleague of Prof. Chen, spent 15 years researching a treatment for his childhood Pokémon — a **Caninos** that suffered from a **degenerative cellular disorder of the pyrogenic glands** (its fire glands were slowly necrotizing — a real-medicine analogue to incurable degenerative diseases).

His research led to a **viral vector therapy**: a modified virus designed to deliver corrective genetic material to diseased cells. This is a real, established medical concept. **Crucially**, his strain was engineered to do more than slow degeneration — it was meant to **stop cellular death entirely** by replacing dying cells with self-perpetuating parasitic activity. The body would stay "alive" indefinitely.

He tested it on his Caninos, who was dying. The experiment took place in his **secret underground laboratory beneath the Pokémon Tower in Lavender Town** — a facility he had dug over years, camouflaged by the burial rites held above. Nobody thought to look beneath the dead.

The result was catastrophic. The Caninos didn't die — but its consciousness dissolved in the process. The original disease, **amplified** by the hyperactive viral vector, spread through the entire organism and **mutated the animal into something that had never existed before**: a brand-new Pokémon. Patient Zero. The Caninos broke through the lab walls in agony, contagious. **Mortimer does not own it.** He has been searching for it ever since.

The transmitted strain — passed through bites — is the unique mixture of the original disease + hyperactive viral vector. **The entire epidemic descends literally from this one sick Caninos.**

### What the player knows initially

**Almost nothing.** The player was in a coma for **2 weeks** during the outbreak. Prof. Chen explains the basics:
- A viral mutation started in Pokémon, then jumped to humans weakly
- It spreads by bite
- Nobody knows where or how it began
- A vaccine works on Pokémon but not humans (yet)
- Pokémon zombies are **everywhere** now — in the grass, the forests, the cities
- Some humans too — but the virus seems weaker on us
- Chen's full research is at his Viridian lab — too far for him to reach now

The player learns the deeper truth (Mortimer, Patient Zero, Team Vector) progressively through Acts 4–5.

## 2. Patient Zero — A new Pokémon

### Concept

A previously-unknown Pokémon born from the failed experiment. **Not a corrupted Caninos** — a genuinely new creature that the world has never seen. Its existence is the proof of what Mortimer accidentally created.

### Working name: **NECRONOS**

(necro + Caninos, scientific feel, no religious connotation)
Alternates if rejected: Lyssark, Vorrak, Mortkine.

### Type

**Fire / Ghost** — formerly fire (Caninos heritage), with a ghost dimension added by cellular abnormalities.

### Appearance

- Quadruped canid silhouette but **broken proportions** — too-long legs, extra joints, hyena-like hunched spine
- Exposed ribcage with **glowing embers inside** (vestige of the necrotizing fire glands, can't fully extinguish or burn)
- Charred fur in patches; flesh that **regenerates and decomposes in a visible loop** on the flanks (texture instability)
- **Three asymmetric red eyes** (uncontrolled cellular replication)
- Oversized hanging jaw, dripping smoking saliva (the active viral vector)
- **Drifting cellular dust trail** in the air around it constantly
- Tail replaced by a **vitreous/crystalline appendage** — the original genetic correction material, crystallized

### Behavior

- The **only** zombie that retains intelligence — not human, but bestial and calculating
- Solitary. Hides in heavily contaminated zones (Lavender underground, Indigo Plateau ruins)
- **Flees** when it senses vaccine on the player — it "knows"
- Exclusive attack: **Vector Bite** — 2 bite marks guaranteed + chance of immediate Rage

### Role in the story

After Mortimer's death at Saffron, Necronos escapes from the Vector lab cage. The player must track it to the Indigo Plateau ruins.

**The ending (canon):**
The player vaccinates Necronos during the final battle. The vaccine works — Necronos slowly reverts to a healthy Caninos. But the original degenerative disease of his pyrogenic glands was never cured — it was only masked by the viral transformation. Without the mutation sustaining him, the disease resumes. The Caninos, exhausted, lies down on Mortimer's symbolic grave near the Plateau. He can finally rest in peace. His fire glands flicker one last time — warm, not burning — and go out.

This is the true ending image: a small, peaceful Caninos curled on a grave, the cellular dust finally still.

## 3. Team Vector

The organization. Named for the **viral vector** that started everything. Logo: DNA double helix pierced by a syringe.

### Mission (internal)

- Recapture Necronos (Mortimer wants to understand / atone / preserve his friend's "soul")
- Loot medical and technical resources to support that research
- Recruit desperate survivors (sick families, chronic patients) by promising them an antidote that doesn't exist yet

The **grunts are not fanatics** — they're desperate people manipulated by Mortimer's promises. This opens the door to morally ambiguous dialogue and the rival arc (see §9).

### Base of Operations: Saffron City

Team Vector occupies the **Silph Co. tower** in Saffron City. The visible floors are a fortified command post. Beneath the building, Mortimer has built a **3-level underground laboratory** — his most advanced facility, dedicated entirely to tracking and recapturing Necronos.

### Boss: Dr. Mortimer

- Brilliant, broken, **not evil**. Doesn't want power. Has been trying to fix his mistake for 4 years and can't, alone.
- Grew up on Cinnabar Island. His first lab and early hospital research are there (degenerative disease studies, pre-outbreak).
- Personality: weary, lucid, almost relieved when the player arrives. Final confrontation is **conversation interrupted by combat**, not an epic fight.
- **Twist:** at the end of the boss fight, he gives the player the last missing piece of the human formula himself. He dies thanking them. He is not the real final boss — Necronos, escaped during the fight, is.

## 4. Bite & Infection mechanics

### The bite count

- Every enemy hit on a Pokémon adds 1 **bite mark** (counter visible: 1/3, 2/3, 3/3)
- At 3/3 → the Pokémon **turns** mid-fight: attacks the player one turn, then flees
- Can be recovered later by encountering the now-zombie Pokémon as a wild zombie and vaccinating it (returns with original stats + the standard cured-Pokémon buff)
- Bite count drops by 1 per day if the Pokémon is rested (in team but not used in combat) — rotation = strategy
- **Anti-Bite item** (rare loot): erases 1 bite mark instantly

### Infection stages (visible in zombie sprites)

| Stage | When | Stats | Sprite hint | Where |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **I — Fresh** | Recently turned | +30% speed, +20% ATK, +HP | Almost-normal, red eyes, fresh drool | Recently fallen cities |
| **II — Stable** | Default | Baseline | Classic zombie — gray, scarred | Most zones |
| **III — Late** | Long-decomposed | -20% speed, -10% ATK, -20% HP | Tattered, hanging limbs | Long-abandoned forests, caves |
| **R — Rage** | Random / "hunger" | +50% ATK, +50% speed, -20% HP | Glowing red eyes, blood streaks | Rare, very dangerous |

**Geographic logic:** cities that fell early (Lavender, Celadon, Saffron) have Stage III zombies — old, decomposed. Cities that fell later have Stage I. The map tells the epidemic's chronology.

**Lavender Town exception:** both Stage III (original zombies from the very beginning) AND Rage (Necronos proximity effect). The worst combination.

## 5. Bite-derived attacks (zombie movesets)

Every zombie has **only one attack**, a variant of Bite based on type.

| Zombie type | Attack | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Z-Rattata, Z-Tauros, basic | **Bite** | Baseline damage + 1 bite mark |
| Z-Charmander, fire types | **Burning Bite** | 1 mark + burn status |
| Z-Grimer, Z-Zubat, poison | **Poisonous Bite** | 1 mark + poison |
| Z-Haunter, Z-Gengar, ghost | **Spectral Bite** | Ignores defenses, 1 mark |
| Z-Geodude, Z-Onix, rock | **Seismic Bite** | 1 mark + chance to stun |
| Z-Pidgey, Z-Tauros (winged) | **Voracious Bite** | 2 marks in one hit (rare) |
| **Patient Zero (Necronos)** | **Vector Bite** | 2 marks guaranteed + drain HP |
| Human zombies | **Bite** (basic) | 1 mark, low damage |

## 6. Hordes

**Visual:** clusters of 4-6 zombie sprites grouped on one tile, visible from a distance.

### Detection
- Manhattan distance ≤ 5 tiles → the horde sees the player
- On-screen alert: ⚠ "A horde spotted you!"
- Persistent red flashing indicator while being chased

### Movement
- The horde moves **1 tile per turn** toward the player
- **1 in 3 hordes are ENRAGED** → they move **2 tiles per turn**. Marked visually with red pulsing border.

### Hiding
- Hide tiles: tall bushes / overturned cars / shelter (specific icon on the map)
- Step on hide tile + press **A** to hide
- You disappear for **3 turns**. The horde loses your trail and walks away.
- A given hide spot only works **once per horde** — they remember it after.

### If the horde catches you
- Multi-zombie battle: 3 zombies in sequence, **no flee allowed**
- Reward: 50% chance of rare loot (medkit, vaccine)
- Your Pokémon take **more bite marks** than usual due to consecutive fights

### Mega-Hordes (zone-blocking)
Some zones are entirely blocked by a **mega-horde** until a specific event disperses it:
- **Cerulean City center**: fuel tank explosion at the port disperses the horde into the sea
- **Celadon City**: controlled explosion of the military armory depot (found via Viridian/Vermilion archives) disperses the horde, giving a limited exploration window

## 7. Immunity — Why some Pokémon survived

### Starters (with Chen)
Chen had heard rumors of Mortimer's research and was suspicious. He started a **preventive immunization protocol** on three test Pokémon (the starters) before the outbreak. That's why he has them, and why they're immune.

### Extreme zones — Natural sanctuaries
The strain is sensitive to **environmental extremes**. It stays dormant or dies in:
- **Deep cold** (Mt. Moon, high mountain routes)
- **Volcanic heat** (Cinnabar Island, volcano summit)

→ Pokémon and humans in those zones are **not infected and do not transform**. These zones are sanctuaries — the player can find normal, catchable Pokémon there. The atmosphere shifts dramatically: calm, quiet, alive.

**This is NOT where vaccine ingredients are found.** Ingredients are found throughout the world — in abandoned houses, on the ground, in loot — accessible and not puzzle-gated.

## 8. Human zombies

- **Much weaker than Pokémon zombies**: 15-25 HP, 5-7 ATK, only basic Bite
- **Never enraged**. The virus is weaker on humans biologically.
- Many die alone before reaching Stage II. Some are found **already dead** in their houses (descriptive only, no combat — sometimes drop loot).
- Found in: cities (rarely on overworld), inside houses (occasionally), as story-required encounters (Chen = first one).

## 9. The cast

### The player
- 12-13-year-old kid (chosen gender boy/girl, custom name)
- Was a normal kid 2 weeks ago
- Father died 5 years ago (mining accident, mentioned briefly, no drama)
- Lives with mother in Pallet Town
- Was in coma for **2 weeks** during outbreak — knows nothing of what happened
- Driving motivation: **save Mom**

### Prof. Chen (`chen`)
- Old colleague of Mortimer, ironically
- Watched over the player during their coma
- Bitten retrieving the player from the forest before the game starts
- Gives starter, explains situation, then transforms — **the player must achieve him personally** (with the starter)
- His final wish: synthesize the human vaccine using his notes at the Viridian lab

### Bertrand (`bertrand`)
- Old man in Pallet Town, side-quest giver
- Trade: 2 rations → 3 vaccines
- Provides post-quest tips about Pokémon types (e.g. ghosts immune to normal)

### Mom (`mother_zombie`)
- Visible at the window of the player's house in Pallet Town from day one
- House is locked (explicit early-game block: "The door is locked. Something is grunting inside.")
- The whole game's goal is to come back with the human vaccine and save her
- **Final emotional encounter** when you return: not a kill fight — you must survive while approaching to inject. Tense, emotional, not violent.

### Dr. Mortimer
- Grew up on Cinnabar Island. His childhood Caninos is what drives everything.
- His early research (degenerative diseases, pre-outbreak) happened at a small lab and hospital on Cinnabar.
- Later moved research to Lavender Town — built a secret underground lab beneath the Pokémon Tower.
- The experiment that created Necronos happened there, alone, in secret.
- After the catastrophe: founded Team Vector, moved to Saffron City (Silph Co. underground lab), has been hunting Necronos for 4 years.
- See §3 above for personality and role.

### The Rival
- Same age as the player
- Joined Team Vector because his little sister was sick — they promised the antidote. He realized it was a scam.
- Crossed 3 times throughout the game:
  - **Route 1**: flees, mistakes player for Vector grunt (player has vaccines)
  - **Lavender Town**: breaks down, confides everything. Turning point.
  - **Saffron City (Vector HQ)**: rejoins for the assault — NPC ally in cutscene, not in team
- His sister's fate: unknown at end of game. Left deliberately open.

### The Pokémon League
- Silent in a bunker at Indigo Plateau
- Sealed the mountain routes to the Plateau from Day 3
- From Act 3 onward, sends encrypted radio transmissions (receivable via repaired antenna in Lavender)
- Sends occasional vaccine drone drops to known survivor camps
- Emerges at the end to begin rebuilding — gives hope to a hopeless world

## 11. The ending

### Canonical ending (bittersweet)
- Mom is saved (vaccine works)
- Necronos is vaccinated → reverts to Caninos → dies peacefully from his original degenerative disease, finally free of the transformation. His fire glands flicker one last time — warm, not burning — and go quiet. He rests on Mortimer's grave.
- The virus stays **endemic in the wild**: the world will never be "before". But humanity survives. Vaccines stockpiled, the League emerges, rebuilding starts.
- Final scene: Mom is awake, weak, kisses you. You look out the window — a wild Pidgey passes. Life resumes, scarred.

### Epilogue variations
- The Rival's sister: found alive in a Vector facility (if player unlocked related clues) or fate unknown
- The Pidgey Boy: joined the Pewter survivor camp (if player chose to bring him) or still in his tree
- The Admiral: leads the naval reconstruction effort
- Bertrand: still in Pallet, eating well

## 12. Tone & writing rules

- **Purely scientific.** No biblical references, no rituals, no mysticism. Real-medicine analogues only.
- **Emotionally grounded.** The drama is in personal stakes (Mom, Chen, Mortimer's grief), not in cosmic threat.
- **Dialogue is sober.** No melodrama. People in apocalypses are tired and pragmatic, not theatrical.
- **Children language stays age-appropriate.** The player is a kid. The world is brutal but the player's voice is confused, scared, determined — not jaded.
- **Mortimer is a tragedy, not a villain.** Every encounter with Vector should hint at this before the reveal.
- **No companion humans on the team.** The rival shows up but stays NPC-only. Pokémon are the partners.
- **Death is quiet.** No epic death speeches. Chen transforms mid-sentence. Mortimer sits down and doesn't get up. Necronos just stops.
